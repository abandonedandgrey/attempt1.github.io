      Abandoned houses are always grey.
      So many days with people in pain,
      All the wail of the moon,
      As it hangs over grey walls.
      With wilted flowers arranged on tile,
      On a blue Saturday afternoon.
      With blue-painted rooms,
      Sitting on counters,
      With dead wires,
      Stripped of our humanity,
      No hands hath trained the ivy,
      To climb those ragged walls,
      And offer us our selves,
      And we've done it to ourselves.
      We've fed the whispers in the air,
      In those walls,
      Our secrets,
      Our nameless lives,
      Along with our fear,
      Our sorrow,
      Our shame,
      In a world that calls us liers,
      And thieves.
